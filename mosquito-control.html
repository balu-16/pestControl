Health Risks

Diseases Prevented by Mosquito Control

Professional mosquito management protects your family from these dangerous diseases.

Dengue Fever

Transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, dengue causes high fever, severe headache, joint pain, and can progress to life-threatening dengue hemorrhagic fever. Cases spike during and after monsoon season.

Malaria

Spread by Anopheles mosquitoes, malaria remains a significant health concern in many parts of India. Symptoms include cyclic fever, chills, and fatigue. Severe cases can be fatal without treatment.

Chikungunya

Also transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, chikungunya causes severe joint pain that can persist for months. The disease has seen major outbreaks across India in recent years.

Japanese Encephalitis

Transmitted by Culex mosquitoes, this viral infection causes brain inflammation. While rare, it can be severe and is particularly dangerous for children.

Our Process

Integrated Mosquito Management

A systematic approach for comprehensive mosquito control.

01

Site Survey

Our technicians thoroughly inspect your property to identify mosquito breeding sites, resting areas, and entry points. We examine drains, water features, plant containers, air coolers, and any potential standing water sources both on your property and nearby.

02

Source Reduction

We treat or eliminate identified breeding sites. This includes applying larvicides to water that cannot be drained, recommending drainage solutions, and advising on habitat modifications to reduce mosquito breeding opportunities around your property.

03

Adult Mosquito Treatment

We apply fogging and/or residual spray treatments to rapidly reduce existing adult mosquito populations. Treatment areas include outdoor vegetation, shaded areas, wall surfaces, and other mosquito resting spots.

04

Ongoing Protection

Mosquito control requires regular maintenance, especially during monsoon season. Our AMC plans include scheduled treatments, breeding site monitoring, and rapid response service during disease outbreaks in your area.
