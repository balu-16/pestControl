Understanding the Problem

The Dangers of Rodent Infestations

Rodents carry 35+ diseases and can damage wiring, plumbing, and structures. A single pair can produce 1,500 offspring yearly. Early professional intervention prevents minor problems from becoming major infestations.

Signs of Rodent Infestation

  • Droppings near food sources, along walls, or in drawers
  • Gnaw marks on food packaging, furniture, or wiring
  • Scratching or scurrying sounds in walls or ceilings at night
  • Greasy rub marks along walls and baseboards
  • Nests made of shredded paper, fabric, or plant matter
  • Ammonia-like smell from rodent urine

Know Your Enemy

Common Rodents We Control

Understanding rodent behavior helps us develop the most effective treatment strategy.

Norway Rat (Sewer Rat)

Large, brown rats that typically burrow underground. Common in basements, ground floors, and sewers. They're excellent swimmers and often enter through drainage systems. Can grow up to 40cm including tail.

Roof Rat (Black Rat)

Agile climbers that prefer elevated areas like attics, ceilings, and upper floors. Slimmer than Norway rats with longer tails. Often enter through roof gaps, overhead wires, or tree branches near buildings.

House Mouse

Small, curious rodents that can survive in minimal spaces. Excellent climbers and jumpers. Their small size allows entry through tiny gaps. Reproduce rapidly and are common in both urban and rural areas.

Bandicoot Rat

Large, aggressive rats common in South India. Known for their burrowing habits and can cause significant structural damage. Often found in gardens, agricultural areas, and near food storage facilities.
